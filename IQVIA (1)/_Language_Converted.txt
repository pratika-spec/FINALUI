Explanations
Based on the diagnosis-related group (DRG) statistics, approximately 16.2 million patients were discharged from inpatient hospital treatment in 2021. A total of 60.7 million surgeries and medical procedures were performed. The number of hospital cases in which a surgery or medical procedure was performed was 14.8 million. On average, 4 measures were taken for each of these cases in 2021.
This publication includes all operation and procedure codes at the OPS-4 level (without duplicates at the codable endpoint level) for inpatient patients discharged from the hospital in the reporting year, broken down by age groups and gender.
Data source:
The survey covers all hospitals that settle according to the DRG reimbursement system and are subject to the scope of the Hospital Remuneration Act (KHEntgG). This includes military hospitals treating civilian patients and clinics of the accident insurance associations where treatment costs are not reimbursed by accident insurance but by health insurance. Excluded are hospitals in the penal or detention system and police hospitals, psychiatric facilities, psychosomatics, and psychotherapeutic medicine facilities.
Gender
The third gender in official statistics - recording and identification of the characteristic manifestations "diverse" and "no information".
To maintain confidentiality, the third gender cannot be published in this publication. The characteristic manifestations "diverse" and "no information" are randomly assigned to the categories "male" and "female" (without proportional quoting, with an expected value of 0.5).
Procedures
All significant surgical interventions and medical procedures that are carried out from admission to discharge and can be mapped in the official operations and procedures code (OPS) are to be coded by the hospitals in accordance with the German coding guidelines. This includes diagnostic, therapeutic, and nursing procedures. The key includes: diagnostic measures, imaging diagnostics, surgeries, non-operative therapeutic measures, medications, and complementary measures. The definition of a significant procedure is that it is either of a surgical nature, carries a risk of intervention or anesthesia, requires special facilities or equipment, or special training. The official version of the operations and procedures code applicable in the reporting year is decisive.
Excursus: OPS
The official operations and procedures code (OPS, until 2004 OPS-301) is used to encode surgeries and other medical procedures in inpatient care and outpatient surgery. It is the official procedure classification for performance records and billing by German hospitals.
The classification has been issued and provided by the Federal Institute for Drugs and Medical Devices (BfArM) since 1993 on behalf of the Federal Ministry of Health, in accordance with §§ 295 and 301 SGB V. The OPS is predominantly numerically and hierarchically structured and has a topographical-anatomical breakdown. A subject-specific breakdown has been omitted. The hierarchical classes include chapters, section headings, 3-digit codes, 4-digit codes, 5-digit codes, and 6-digit codes. In some areas, an alphanumeric notation or hierarchical structure is used, including for the designation of the residual classes "Other surgeries" (supplemented with the identifier "x") and "Not otherwise specified surgeries" (supplemented with the identifier "y").
Note on multiple entries:
For the representation of complex procedures and partial measures, the coding of surgeries with multiple codes is provided in various areas. Furthermore, the treatment of intraoperative complications is to be encoded separately. Therefore, the publication shows multiple codings (without duplicates) per treatment case.
You can find the 2021 version of the operations and procedures code on the website of the Federal Institute for Drugs and Medical Devices (BfArM) at https://www.dimdi.de/static/de/klassifikationen/ops/kode-suche/opshtml2021/. They can be downloaded there for free in various storage formats.Health
